## Buying Guide: How to Buy Gold & Silver Smartly

Whether you are buying for a special occasion or investment, keep these points in mind to avoid overpaying.

### 1. Check the "Spot Price" vs. "Making Charges"
The rates quoted above are the "spot prices" or raw material costs. Jewellers add **making charges** (labor costs) on top of this.
*   **Machine-made jewellery:** Usually has lower making charges (₹ 300–₹ 500 per gram).
*   **Handmade jewellery:** Can go up to ₹ 1,000+ per gram.
*   **Tip:** Always ask for a breakdown of the bill separating the gold cost from the making charges.

### 2. Verify Purity (Hallmarking)
In India, **BIS Hallmarking** is mandatory for gold jewellery.
*   Look for the BIS logo and the caratage (22K916, 18K750).
*   In 2026, hallmarking rules are stricter; ensure every piece has a 6-digit Unique ID number.

### 3. Understand GST
Remember that a **3% GST** (Goods and Services Tax) is levied on the final bill (including making charges). This is separate from the base gold price.

### 4. Digital Gold vs. Physical Gold
*   **Physical Gold:** Tangible asset, good for emotional security and weddings.
*   **Digital Gold/Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs):** Better for pure investment as there are no making charges or storage costs. SGBs also offer an annual interest rate (2.5%) over the gold price appreciation.
